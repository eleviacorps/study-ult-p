## Formula Relationships

### From Systems of Units:
- Unit conversion → Used in all calculations
- SI prefixes → Applied in scientific notation

### From Dimensional Analysis:
- [Dimensions] → Used to derive relationships
- Homogeneity principle → Checks formula correctness
- Constant dimensions → Find [G], [h], [kB]

### From Significant Figures:
- Rounding rules → Applied to final answers
- SF in operations → Determines precision

### From Errors:

- Error reduction → Multiple readings

- Least count → Determines measurement precision

---

## Cross-Topic Links

### Unit Conversion ↔ Dimensional Analysis
- **How connected:** Both deal with consistency of physical quantities
- **Link:** Converting units is essentially dimensional analysis in practice

**Example:** Converting km/h to m/s uses understanding of dimensions (length/time)

---

### Significant Figures ↔ Errors
- **How connected:** Both deal with measurement precision
- **Link:** SF reflects uncertainty, errors quantify it

**Example:** When calculating error, use SF rules to report final answer

---

### Dimensional Analysis ↔ Errors
- **How connected:** Both use fundamental quantities
- **Link:** Dimensions of error terms must match

**Example:** When finding dimension of error, use same dimension analysis

---

### Measuring Instruments ↔ Errors
- **How connected:** Instruments determine error magnitude
- **Link:** Least count directly gives error in measurement

**Example:** Vernier LC = 0.1 mm → minimum error is ±0.05 mm

---

### Unit Conversion ↔ Significant Figures
- **How connected:** Both affect final numerical value
- **Link:** After unit conversion, must apply SF rules

**Example:** Convert 72 km/h to m/s = 20 m/s (2 SF because of 3.6)

## Practical Application Connections

### In Mechanics:
- Unit conversion: km/h ↔ m/s
- Dimensional analysis: Check force equations
- Error analysis: Experimental uncertainties

### In Thermal Physics:
- Temperature conversion: °C ↔ K
- Significant figures: Heat capacity calculations
- Errors: Temperature measurement uncertainty

### In Waves/Optics:
- Dimensionless quantities: Refractive index, angle
- Significant figures: Wavelength measurements

### In Electricity:
- Dimensional analysis: Check circuit formulas
- Unit conversions: eV to joules
