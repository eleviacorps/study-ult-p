# Common Mistakes - Units, Dimensions and Measurement

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> This chapter has specific traps that cost students marks. Learn to avoid them!

---

## 1. Unit Conversion Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Forgetting Time Conversion in Speed
**Error:** Converting km to m but leaving hour as hour
**Wrong:** 72 km/h = 72 m/s
**Correct:** 72 km/h = 20 m/s (divide by 3.6)

**How to Avoid:** Always write full conversion: (72 × 1000 m) / (3600 s)

---

### Mistake 2: Confusing Mass and Weight
**Error:** Treating "weight" as mass
**Wrong:** My weight is 60 kg
**Correct:** My mass is 60 kg, my weight is 588 N (on Earth)

**How to Avoid:** Remember: weight is a force (N), mass is quantity (kg)

---

### Mistake 3: Wrong Temperature Conversion
**Error:** Adding/subtracting wrong value from Celsius
**Correct:** K = °C + 273 (or 273.15 for precision)

**How to Avoid:** 273 is for JEE, 273.15 for precise work

---

## 2. Significant Figures Mistakes

### Mistake 4: Counting Leading Zeros
**Error:** 0.00520 has 5 significant figures
**Correct:** 0.00520 has 3 significant figures (5, 2, 0)

**How to Avoid:** "Leading zeros don't count - they're just placeholders"

---

### Mistake 5: Using Wrong Rule for Addition
**Error:** Using least significant figures for addition
**Wrong:** 12.11 + 3.1345 → 15.2 (used 2 SF)
**Correct:** 15.24 (used 2 decimal places)

**How to Avoid:** Remember: Addition uses DECIMAL places, multiplication uses SF

---

### Mistake 6: Premature Rounding
**Error:** Rounding intermediate calculations
**Correct:** Keep full precision until final answer

**How to Avoid:** Write all digits through calculation, round at end

---

### Mistake 7: Ambiguous Trailing Zeros
**Error:** Assuming 1500 has 4 SF
**Correct:** Write as 1500. or 1.500 × 10³ for clarity

**How to Avoid:** Use scientific notation when in doubt

---

## 3. Dimensional Analysis Mistakes

### Mistake 8: Adding Quantities with Different Dimensions
**Error:** Writing v = u + a (dimensionally wrong)
**Correct:** v = u + at (all terms have LT⁻¹)

**How to Avoid:** Check dimensions BEFORE solving

---

### Mistake 9: Forgetting Negative Exponents
**Error:** Writing [MLT] instead of [MLT⁻¹]
**Correct:** Velocity = [ML⁰T⁻¹], Acceleration = [ML⁰T⁻²]

**How to Avoid:** Write dimension with proper exponents from start

---

### Mistake 10: Ignoring Dimensionless Constants
**Error:** Thinking π or 2 affects dimensions
**Correct:** Constants like π, 2, e are dimensionless

**How to Avoid:** Ignore numerical constants in dimensional analysis

---

### Mistake 11: Confusing Dimensionless with Unitless
**Error:** Saying angle has no dimensions
**Correct:** Angle has dimensions of ratio (L/L = dimensionless), but uses "radian" as unit

**How to Avoid:** Remember: radian is a "dimensionless unit"

---

## 4. Error Analysis Mistakes

### Mistake 12: Subtracting Errors in Subtraction
**Error:** Δ(A-B) = ΔA - ΔB
**Correct:** Δ(A-B) = ΔA + ΔB (errors always ADD!)

**How to Avoid:** "SUMDIFF = ADD" - Sum OR Difference, errors ADD

---

### Mistake 13: Forgetting Absolute Value in Power
**Error:** For A², ΔA²/A² = -2 × ΔA/A
**Correct:** ΔA²/A² = 2 × ΔA/A (use absolute value)

**How to Avoid:** Error magnitude is always positive

---

### Mistake 14: Too Many SF in Error
**Error:** Reporting error as ±0.1234567
**Correct:** Report error to 1-2 SF only (±0.12)

**How to Avoid:** Error has limited precision - 1 or 2 SF maximum

---

### Mistake 15: Confusing Absolute and Relative Error
**Error:** "The relative error is 2 cm"
**Correct:** "The relative error is 0.02" or "2%"

**How to Avoid:** Relative error is a ratio (no units!)

---

## 5. Instrument Measurement Mistakes

### Mistake 16: Forgetting Zero Error
**Error:** Taking reading without checking zero error
**Correct:** ALWAYS check and correct for zero error

**How to Avoid:** Make zero error check your first step

---

### Mistake 17: Wrong Sign in Zero Error Correction
**Error:** Adding when should subtract or vice versa
**Correct:** Positive = subtract, Negative = add

**How to Avoid:** "Positive = Subtract, Negative = Add"

---

### Mistake 18: Confusing VSD and MSD
**Error:** Counting divisions incorrectly
**Correct:** Note which VSD division exactly coincides with MSD

**How to Avoid:** The coinciding division is exactly aligned - no guessing

---

### Mistake 19: Wrong LC Formula Application
**Error:** Using (n-1)/n instead of 1/n
**Correct:** LC = (1 MSD)/n where n = VSD divisions

**How to Avoid:** For standard vernier: 10 VSD → LC = 0.1 mm

---

### Mistake 20: Mixing Sleeve and Thimble in Screw Gauge
**Error:** Reading circular scale as mm
**Correct:** Sleeve = mm, thimble = fraction of mm

**How to Avoid:** Remember: "Sleeve gives whole, thimble gives fraction"

---

## Quick Reference: Common Errors Summary

| Category | Common Error | Correct Approach |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Units | Wrong conversion factor | km/h ÷ 3.6 = m/s |
| SF | Wrong rule for operation | Addition → decimal, multiplication → SF |
| Dimensions | Adding unlike quantities | Check dimensions first |
| Errors | Subtracting in subtraction | Always add errors |
| Instruments | Forgetting zero error | Check first, correct at end |
