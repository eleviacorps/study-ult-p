# Memory Techniques - Units, Dimensions and Measurement

---

## Mnemonic 1: SI Base Units

**Mnemonic:** "Ma Li Ti Am Ke Mo Ca"

**What it stands for:**
- Ma = Mass (kilogram)
- Li = Length (metre)
- Ti = Time (second)
- Am = Ampere (current)
- Ke = Kelvin (temperature)
- Mo = Mole (amount)
- Ca = Candela (luminous intensity)

**How to use:** First letters form a memorable word

---

## Mnemonic 2: SI Prefixes - Large

**Mnemonic:** "King Henry Died By Drinking Chocolate Milk"

**What it stands for:**
- K = Kilo (10³)
- H = Hecto (10²)
- D = Deka (10¹)
- B = Base (10⁰)
- D = Deci (10⁻¹)
- C = Centi (10⁻²)
- M = Milli (10⁻³)

**How to use:** Remember this sentence to recall order and powers

---

## Mnemonic 3: Unit Conversions

**Mnemonic:** "3.6 for Speed"

**What it stands for:**
- km/h → m/s: Divide by 3.6
- m/s → km/h: Multiply by 3.6

**Why it works:** 3600 seconds in hour ÷ 1000 meters in km = 3.6

**Additional:**
- g/cm³ → kg/m³: Multiply by 1000
- °C → K: Add 273

---

## Mnemonic 4: Error in Operations

**Mnemonic:** "SUMDIFF = ADD"

**What it stands for:**
- SUM or DIFFerence → errors always ADD
- PRODUCT or QUOTIENT → relative errors ADD
- POWER → exponent multiplies relative error

**Full mnemonic:** "Sum Diff, errors Add; Prod Quot, relative Add; Power multiplies"

---

## Mnemonic 5: Zero Error Correction

**Mnemonic:** "Positive Subtracts, Negative Adds"

**What it stands for:**
- Positive zero error → subtract to correct
- Negative zero error → add to correct

**Visual:** Think of a number line
- Positive error → reading is too HIGH → subtract to lower
- Negative error → reading is too LOW → add to raise

---

## Mnemonic 6: Significant Figures Rules

**Mnemonic:** "LARS for Addition, LSSSF for Multiplication"

**What it stands for:**
- LARS = Least Absolute Rounding by Scale (decimal places) for addition
- LSSSF = Least Significant Significant Figures for multiplication/division

**How to use:** Different operations use different precision rules

---

## Mnemonic 7: Leading Zeros

**Mnemonic:** "Leading are Lazy"

**What it stands for:**
- Leading zeros are NEVER significant
- They're just placeholders showing decimal position

**Example:** 0.00520 → 5, 2, 0 are significant; zeros before 5 are lazy

---

## Mnemonic 8: Dimensional Formula - Force

**Mnemonic:** "MaLTea" (read as "Malt tea")

**What it stands for:**
- M = Mass
- a (first letter in formula) = times
- L = Length
- Tea = T⁻² (T with exponent -2)

**How to use:** Say "Ma L Tea" to remember Force = M × L × T⁻²

---

## Mnemonic 9: Vernier LC Formula

**Mnemonic:** "One Over N"

**What it stands for:**
- LC = 1 MSD / n
- n = number of VSD divisions

**Why it works:** Simple phrase to remember formula structure

---

## Mnemonic 10: Screw Gauge LC

**Mnemonic:** "Pitch Over Divisions"

**What it stands for:**
- LC = Pitch / divisions on thimble

**How to use:** Picture dividing the pitch (distance moved per rotation) by divisions

## Association Techniques

### Link 1: Error Addition → Always Adding
Think of shopping - when you have errors in price AND tax, they BOTH add to final cost.

### Link 2: Negative Exponents → "In the Denominator"
Remember: T⁻¹ means 1/T in denominator

### Link 3: Dimensionless → "No M, L, or T"
If you can't find M, L, or T in the dimensions, it's dimensionless!

---

## Visual Anchors

### Anchor 1: The Staircase
Imagine a staircase:
- Going UP (larger to smaller unit): Multiply by powers of 10
- Going DOWN (smaller to larger unit): Divide by powers of 10
- The higher you go, the smaller your units

### Anchor 2: The Balance Scale
Think of dimensions balancing like a scale:
- If equation has mass on one side, must have mass on other
- If unbalanced, equation is WRONG

### Anchor 3: Thermometer Scale
Remember temperature conversion:
- Think of thermometer: more K = higher, so add 273
