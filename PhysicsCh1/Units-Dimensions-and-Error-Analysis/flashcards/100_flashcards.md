# 100+ Flashcards - Units, Dimensions and Measurement

## FC1. SI System Base Units
**Topic:** Systems of Units

**Q:** Name the seven fundamental SI quantities and their units.

**A:** 1. Mass - kilogram (kg)
2. Length - metre (m)
3. Time - second (s)
4. Electric current - ampere (A)
5. Temperature - kelvin (K)
6. Amount of substance - mole (mol)
7. Luminous intensity - candela (cd)

**Memory Trick:** "Ma Li Ti Am Ke Mo Ca" (First letters)

---

## FC2. Unit Conversion - Speed
**Topic:** Unit Conversion

**Q:** Convert km/h to m/s and vice versa.

**A:** km/h → m/s: divide by 3.6
m/s → km/h: multiply by 3.6

**Example:** 72 km/h = 72/3.6 = 20 m/s

**Memory Trick:** "3-6-0-0" - 3600 seconds in hour, 1000 meters in km

---

## FC3. Least Count Formula - Vernier
**Topic:** Vernier Calipers

**Q:** Formula for least count of vernier calipers.

**A:** LC = (1 MSD) / n
where n = number of VSD divisions

**Example:** 10 VSD = 9 MSD → LC = 1mm/10 = 0.1mm

---

## FC4. Least Count Formula - Screw Gauge
**Topic:** Screw Gauge

**Q:** Formula for least count of screw gauge.

**A:** LC = Pitch / Number of divisions on thimble

**Example:** Pitch = 0.5mm, 100 divisions → LC = 0.5/100 = 0.005mm

---

## FC5. Dimensional Formula - Force
**Topic:** Dimensional Analysis

**Q:** Dimensional formula of force.

**A:** [M¹ L¹ T⁻²]

**Derivation:** Force = mass × acceleration = M × LT⁻² = MLT⁻²

## FC6. Dimensional Formula - Energy

**Q:** Dimensional formula of work/energy.

**A:** [M¹ L² T⁻²]

**Derivation:** Work = Force × distance = MLT⁻² × L = ML²T⁻²

## FC7. Dimensional Formula - Pressure

## FC8. Significant Figures Rules - Leading Zeros
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** Are leading zeros significant?

**A:** NO - Leading zeros (zeros to the left of first non-zero digit) are NEVER significant.

**Example:** 0.00520 has 3 SF (5, 2, 0)

---

## FC9. Significant Figures Rules - Trailing Zeros
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** Are trailing zeros significant?

**A:** It depends:
- With decimal point: YES (2.500 has 4 SF)
- Without decimal point: AMBIGUOUS (1500 could be 1, 2, or 4 SF)

**Solution:** Use scientific notation to clarify

---

## FC10. Error Addition - Sum
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = A + B, what is ΔZ?

**A:** ΔZ = ΔA + ΔB (absolute errors ADD)

**Example:** A = 10 ± 1, B = 5 ± 1 → A+B = 15 ± 2

---

## FC11. Error Addition - Difference
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = A - B, what is ΔZ?

**A:** ΔZ = ΔA + ΔB (errors ADD even for subtraction!)

**Example:** A = 10 ± 1, B = 5 ± 1 → A-B = 5 ± 2

**Memory Trick:** "SUMDIFF = ADD" - Sum OR Difference, errors ADD

---

## FC12. Error in Product
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = A × B, what is ΔZ/Z?

**A:** ΔZ/Z = ΔA/A + ΔB/B (Relative errors ADD in multiplication)

**Example:** A = 10 ± 1 (10%), B = 5 ± 1 (20%) → ΔZ/Z = 0.1 + 0.2 = 0.3

---

## FC13. Error in Division
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = A/B, what is ΔZ/Z?

**A:** ΔZ/Z = ΔA/A + ΔB/B (Relative errors ADD in division)

**Same as multiplication!**

---

## FC14. Error in Power
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = Aⁿ, what is ΔZ/Z?

**A:** ΔZ/Z = |n| × ΔA/A (Relative error multiplied by exponent)

**Example:** If radius has 2% error, volume of sphere has 6% error (3 × 2%)

---

## FC15. Zero Error - Vernier Positive
**Topic:** Vernier Calipers

**Q:** What is positive zero error and how to correct?

**A:** When VSD zero is to the RIGHT of MSD zero → reading is too HIGH
**Correction:** Subtract (VSD reading × LC) from measured value

---

## FC16. Zero Error - Vernier Negative
**Topic:** Vernier Calipers

**Q:** What is negative zero error and how to correct?

**A:** When VSD zero is to the LEFT of MSD zero → reading is too LOW
**Correction:** Add (VSD reading × LC) to measured value

**Memory Trick:** "Positive = Subtract, Negative = Add"

---

## FC17. Dimensional Homogeneity
**Topic:** Dimensional Analysis

**Q:** What is principle of dimensional homogeneity?

**A:** In any physically meaningful equation, all terms must have the same dimensions.

**Example:** v = u + at → [v] = [u] = [at] = LT⁻¹ ✓

---

## FC18. Cannot Find by Dimensional Analysis
**Topic:** Dimensional Analysis

**Q:** What CANNOT be determined by dimensional analysis?

**A:** 1. Value of dimensionless constants (π, e, 2, etc.)
2. Distinction between quantities with same dimensions (torque vs energy)

**Memory Trick:** "Dimless Constants Are Invisible" to dimensional analysis

---

## FC19. Relative Error Formula
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** Formula for relative error.

**A:** δx = Δx / x

**Percentage Error:** δx × 100%

---

## FC20. Unit Conversion - Density
**Topic:** Unit Conversion

**Q:** Convert g/cm³ to kg/m³ and vice versa.

**A:** g/cm³ → kg/m³: multiply by 1000
kg/m³ → g/cm³: divide by 1000

**Example:** 2.7 g/cm³ = 2700 kg/m³

## FC21. Number of SF - Rules
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** What are the 5 rules for counting significant figures?

**A:** 1. Non-zero digits are always significant
2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
3. Leading zeros are never significant
4. Trailing zeros after decimal ARE significant
5. Trailing zeros without decimal are AMBIGUOUS

---

## FC22. Rounding Rules
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** How do you round when last digit is exactly 5?

**A:** Round to nearest EVEN number

**Example:**
3.745 → 3.74 (4 is even)
3.755 → 3.76 (6 is even)
3.7450 → 3.74 (5 followed by zero)

---

## FC23. SF in Addition/Subtraction
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** What determines precision in addition/subtraction?

**A:** Decimal places (NOT significant figures)

**Example:** 12.11 + 3.1345 → 12.11 has 2 decimal places → answer to 2 decimal places

---

## FC24. SF in Multiplication/Division
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** What determines precision in multiplication/division?

**A:** Significant figures (NOT decimal places)

**Example:** 4.56 × 1.4 → 4.56 has 3 SF, 1.4 has 2 SF → answer to 2 SF

---

## FC31. CGS to SI - Force
**Topic:** Unit Conversion

**Q:** 1 dyne = ? N

**A:** 1 dyne = 10⁻⁵ N

**Derivation:** 1 dyne = 1 g × 1 cm/s² = 10⁻³ kg × 10⁻² m/s² = 10⁻⁵ N

---

## FC32. CGS to SI - Energy
**Topic:** Unit Conversion

**Q:** 1 erg = ? J

**A:** 1 erg = 10⁻⁷ J

**Derivation:** 1 erg = 1 dyne × 1 cm = 10⁻⁵ N × 10⁻² m = 10⁻⁷ J

## FC33. Temperature Conversion
**Topic:** Unit Conversion

**Q:** Convert Celsius to Kelvin and vice versa.

**A:** K = °C + 273 (or 273.15)
°C = K - 273

**Example:** 27°C = 300 K

---

## FC34. Random vs Systematic Errors
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** Difference between random and systematic errors.

**A:** Random errors: Fluctuate randomly, reduced by taking multiple readings
Systematic errors: Consistent shift in one direction, identified and corrected

**Example:** Random - reading variation; Systematic - zero error

---

## FC35. Error in Square Root
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** If Z = √A, what is ΔZ/Z?

**A:** ΔZ/Z = ½ × ΔA/A

**General:** For Z = A^(1/n), ΔZ/Z = (1/n) × ΔA/A

---

## FC36. Significant Figures - Exact Numbers
**Topic:** Significant Figures

**Q:** How are exact numbers treated in SF calculations?

**A:** Exact numbers (counted, defined, or from formulas like 2π) have INFINITE significant figures and don't limit the precision.

**Example:** In A = πr², π is exact → use SF of r only

---

## FC37. Dimensional Analysis Uses
**Topic:** Dimensional Analysis

**Q:** What are the applications of dimensional analysis?

**A:** 1. Check dimensional correctness of equations
2. Derive relationships between physical quantities
3. Find dimensions of constants
4. Convert between unit systems

---

## FC38. Error Reduction by Averaging
**Topic:** Errors

**Q:** How does taking n readings reduce random error?

**A:** Random error reduces by factor of √n

**Example:** 4 readings reduce error by half (√4 = 2)
