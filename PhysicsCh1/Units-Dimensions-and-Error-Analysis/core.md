# Units, Dimensions and Measurement

## Chapter Overview

This chapter forms the foundation of Physics. It deals with the measurement of physical quantities, understanding of SI units, dimensional analysis, and error handling in experiments. This is a **high-weightage chapter for JEE** — appearing almost every year in some form. Mastery of this chapter is non-negotiable because every Physics problem ultimately depends on correct unit conversion and dimensional reasoning.

Key themes covered:
- **Units and Systems of Measurement** (SI, CGS, MKS, FPS)
- **Dimensional Analysis** — derivation and checking of formulae
- **Significant Figures and Rounding**
- **Errors in Measurement** — absolute, relative, and percentage error
- **Practical Applications** —vernier calipers, screw gauge, stopwatch

## Important Topics (High-Scoring & Trap-Heavy)

**High-Scoring:**
- Dimensional formula writing
- Using dimensions to derive relationships
- Error combination in addition/multiplication
- Finding least count of instruments

**Trap-Heavy:**
- Confusion between dimensions and units
- Treating dimensionless quantities as dimensional
- Sign errors in error propagation
- Wrong application of significant figure rules in calculations

## Quick Formula Reference

| Concept | Key Formula |
|---------|------------|
| Dimensional formula of force | $[M^1 L^1 T^{-2}]$ |
| Error in sum | $\Delta Z = \Delta A + \Delta B$ |
| Error in product | $\frac{\Delta Z}{Z} = \frac{\Delta A}{A} + \frac{\Delta B}{B}$ |
| Least count (vernier) | $1 MSD - 1 VSD$ |
| Least count (screw gauge) | $\frac{pitch}{no. of divisions on thimble}$ |
