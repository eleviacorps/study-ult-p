## The Universal Language of Science

### The Big Question

> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> **Why is the SI system the global standard? Because science requires a universal language — when a physicist in Japan says "5 meters," a physicist in America must understand exactly the same distance.**

## 1. History of SI System

### 1.1 Evolution

| Year | Development |
|------|-------------|
| 1790 | French Academy creates metric system |
| 1799 | Prototype meter and kilogram created |
| 1875 | Treaty of the Meter (17 countries) |
| 1948 | MKSA system (Meter, Kilogram, Second, Ampere) |
| 1960 | SI officially adopted |
| 2019 | Redefinition based on fundamental constants |

### 1.2 The 2019 Revolution

> [!INTUISION]
> **Big Change:** In 2019, all SI units were redefined based on fundamental constants rather than physical objects. This means:
> - The kilogram is now defined by Planck's constant
> - The meter is defined by the speed of light
> - The second is defined by cesium radiation

**Why this matters:** Physical objects can change (lose atoms, get damaged), but universal constants NEVER change!

## 2. Complete SI Unit Definitions

### 2.1 Base Unit Definitions (2019)

| Unit | Definition | Nature |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Meter (m)** | The distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 of a second | Based on c (speed of light) |
| **Kilogram (kg)** | Defined by taking the fixed numerical value of Planck's constant h to be 6.62607015 × 10⁻³⁴ | Based on h (Planck's constant) |
| **Second (s)** | 9,192,631,770 periods of radiation from cesium-133 atom | Based on atomic properties |
| **Ampere (A)** | Current that produces a force of 2 × 10⁻⁷ N/m between two parallel wires | Based on electromagnetic force |
| **Kelvin (K)** | 1/273.16 of thermodynamic temperature of triple point of water | Based on water properties |
| **Mole (mol)** | Contains 6.02214076 × 10²³ elementary entities | Based on Avogadro's number |
| **Candela (cd)** | Luminous intensity of source with frequency 540 × 10¹² Hz and power 1/683 W/sr | Based on human eye response |

## 3. Coherent Derived Units

### 3.1 What Makes SI "Coherent"?

> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> A **coherent system** is one where derived units are formed without any numerical factor other than 1. This is why SI is so elegant — no conversion factors needed within the system!

**Example:**
- 1 N (newton) = 1 kg × 1 m/s² (no factor!)
- 1 J (joule) = 1 N × 1 m (no factor!)
- 1 Pa (pascal) = 1 N/m² (no factor!)

**Compare with CGS:**
- 1 dyne = 1 g × 1 cm/s² (different base units!)
- Conversion factor needed: 1 N = 10⁵ dyne

### 3.2 Practical Implications

**Advantages:**
- Easy to convert between related units
- Calculations simpler (no conversion factors)
- Fewer numerical constants to remember
- International acceptance

## 4. Writing Units Correctly

### 4.1 Rules

> [!IMPORTANT]
> 1. **Symbols are lowercase** (except at start of sentence): meter = m, second = s
2. **Named units capitalized** (after person names): Newton = N, Joule = J
3. **Plural is NOT indicated by 's'**: 5 kg NOT 5 kgs
4. **No period** after unit symbols (except end of sentence)
5. **Space between number and unit**: 5 m NOT 5m
6. **Use dot for multiplication**: N·m NOT Nm (to avoid confusion with millinewton)

### 4.2 Correct vs Incorrect

| Incorrect | Correct | Reason |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| 5kg | 5 kg | Space required |
| 5 kgs | 5 kg | No plural 's' |
| 10 sec | 10 s | Use symbol |
| 10 secs | 10 s | No plural 's' |
| Meter | m | Use symbol |
| 5ml | 5 mL | Capital L for liter |
| Newton | N | Capitalize when named |
| m/sec | m/s | Use / not word |

### 5.2 Quick Conversion Tricks

> [!TIP]
> **Converting between prefixes:**
> - Moving UP in table (larger): Divide by power of 10
> - Moving DOWN in table (smaller): Multiply by power of 10
> 
> **Example:** 5 km → mm
> - km → m: 5 × 10³ = 5000 m
> - m → mm: 5000 × 10³ = 5 × 10⁶ mm

## 6. Special Cases and Conventions

### 6.1 Unit for Plane Angle

- **Unit:** Radian (rad)
- **Dimension:** Dimensionless
- **Conversion:** 360° = 2π rad
- **Formula:** θ (rad) = arc/radius

### 6.2 Unit for Solid Angle

- **Unit:** Steradian (sr)
- **Dimension:** Dimensionless
- **Maximum solid angle:** 4π sr (entire sphere)

### 6.3 Unit for Light

- **Luminous flux:** Lumen (lm)
- **Illuminance:** Lux (lx) = lm/m²

## 8. JEE Important Points

### 8.1 Common Conversions to Remember

1. **Astronomical:** 1 AU = 1.5 × 10¹¹ m, 1 ly = 9.46 × 10¹⁵ m
2. **Atomic:** 1 Å = 10⁻¹⁰ m, 1 fm = 10⁻¹⁵ m
3. **Pressure:** 1 atm = 10⁵ Pa (approximately)
4. **Energy:** 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J

### 8.2 Common Mistakes

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> ❌ **Mistake:** Using 'cc' (cubic centimeter) in calculations
> ✅ **Correct:** Convert to m³ (1 cc = 10⁻⁶ m³)
> **Why:** SI requires standard units

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> ❌ **Mistake:** Confusing 'kg' (kilogram) with 'g' (gram)
> ✅ **Correct:** kg is the SI base unit, g is not
> **Why:** The base unit for mass is kg, not g

## 9. Formula Summary

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| Base units | 7 fundamental: m, kg, s, K, A, cd, mol |
| Derived units | All other units expressed in base units |
| Coherent system | No numerical factors within SI |
| Unit symbols | Lowercase except named units |
| Prefixes | Powers of 10, always written with unit |
