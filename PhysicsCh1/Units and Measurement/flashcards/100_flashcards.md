## FC1. What is a Physical Quantity?
**Topic:** [[Introduction to Units and Measurements]]

### Question:
What is a physical quantity?

### Answer:
A property of matter or energy that can be measured quantitatively. Examples: length, mass, time, temperature.

### Formula:
Physical Quantity = Numerical Value × Unit

---

## FC2. Fundamental vs Derived
**Topic:** [[Fundamental and Derived Quantities]]

### Question:
What is the difference between fundamental and derived quantities?

### Answer:
Fundamental quantities are independent and cannot be expressed in terms of other quantities (7 in SI). Derived quantities can be expressed in terms of fundamental quantities (e.g., speed = length/time).

---

## FC3. SI Base Units
**Topic:** [[SI Units System]]

### Question:
Name all 7 SI base units.

### Answer:
Meter (m) - Length, Kilogram (kg) - Mass, Second (s) - Time, Kelvin (K) - Temperature, Ampere (A) - Current, Candela (cd) - Luminous intensity, Mole (mol) - Amount of substance.

---

## FC4. Dimensional Formula
**Topic:** [[Dimensional Analysis]]

### Question:
What is dimensional formula?

### Answer:
Expression showing how a physical quantity depends on fundamental quantities. Example: [Force] = MLT⁻²

---

## FC5. Least Count
**Topic:** [[Introduction to Units and Measurements]]

### Question:
Define least count.

### Answer:
Smallest value that can be measured accurately by an instrument. LC = value of one main scale division / number of vernier/scale divisions.

---

## FC6. Significant Figures Rules
**Topic:** [[Significant Figures]]

### Question:
What are the rules for significant figures?

### Answer:
1. All non-zero digits are significant. 2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant. 3. Leading zeros are never significant. 4. Trailing zeros in decimal are significant.

---

## FC7. Error Types
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
Differentiate between systematic and random errors.

### Answer:
Systematic errors are consistent (always high or always low), can be eliminated by calibration. Random errors fluctuate above/below true value, reduced by averaging.

---

## FC8. Absolute Error
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
How to calculate absolute error?

### Answer:
For single measurement: LC/2. For multiple measurements: (max - min)/2

---

## FC9. Relative Error
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
What is relative error?

### Answer:
Ratio of absolute error to measured value. Relative error = Δx/x (dimensionless). Percentage error = (Δx/x) × 100%

---

## FC10. Error Propagation Addition
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
How do errors propagate in addition?

### Answer:
For z = x ± y, Δz = Δx + Δy (absolute errors add)

---

## FC11. Error Propagation Multiplication
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
How do errors propagate in multiplication?

### Answer:
For z = xy or x/y, relative errors add: Δz/z = Δx/x + Δy/y

---

## FC12. Error in Powers
**Topic:** [[Error Analysis]]

### Question:
If z = xⁿ, what is error propagation?

### Answer:
Relative error multiplies by n: Δz/z = |n| × (Δx/x)

---
