## Ultra-Condensed Notes

### 1. Electric Charge
- Two types: + and -
- Properties: Quantization (q = ne), Additivity, Conservation
- e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C

### 2. Coulomb's Law
$$F = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$$
- k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²
- Force is repulsive for like charges, attractive for unlike

### 3. Electric Field
$$E = \frac{F}{q} = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$$
- Direction: away from +, toward for -
- Unit: N/C or V/m (equivalent)

### 4. Gauss's Law
$$\Phi = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\varepsilon_0}$$
- Only enclosed charge matters!
- Use for symmetric charge distributions

### 5. Applications of Gauss's Law
- Infinite line: $E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 r}$
- Infinite sheet: $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}$
- Sphere outside: $E = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$
- Sphere inside: $E = 0$ (hollow)

### 6. Electric Dipole
- Dipole moment: $p = q \times 2a$
- Direction: from -q to +q
- Field on axis: $E = \frac{2p}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^3}$
- Field on equator: $E = \frac{p}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^3}$
- Torque: $\tau = pE \sin\theta$
- Potential energy: $U = -pE \cos\theta$

## Must-Remember Formulas

### Key Formulas (Quick List)
1. **Coulomb's Law**: $F = \frac{9 \times 10^9 q_1 q_2}{r^2}$
2. **Electric Field**: $E = \frac{9 \times 10^9 Q}{r^2}$
3. **Gauss's Law**: $\Phi = \frac{q}{\varepsilon_0}$
4. **Dipole Moment**: $p = q \times 2a$
5. **Dipole Field (axis)**: $E = \frac{2p}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^3}$
6. **Dipole Field (equator)**: $E = \frac{p}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r^3}$
7. **Torque**: $\tau = pE \sin\theta$
8. **Line Charge Field**: $E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 r}$
9. **Sheet Field**: $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}$

## Last-Minute Points

1. **Like charges repel, unlike attract** — Always remember this!
2. **Electric field inside hollow shell = 0** — Key result from Gauss's Law
3. **Field due to infinite sheet is independent of distance** — Important!
4. **Dipole field falls as 1/r³** — Slower than point charge (1/r²)
5. **Force on charge in field: F = qE** — Don't mix up with E = F/q
6. **Electric flux through closed surface = q/ε₀** — Gauss's Law
7. **Dipole in uniform field experiences torque but no net force** — Important distinction!
8. **For non-uniform field, dipole experiences both force and torque**
9. **Quantization: All charges are integer multiples of e** — q = ne
10. **Conservation: Total charge of isolated system remains constant**

## Memory Triggers

### Quick Mnemonics
- **"PEA"**: Positive pushes Electric field Away
- **"PEN"**: Positive means Electron deficiency (Negative means Excess)
- **"G-L-A-S-S"**: Gauss's Law relates Area (S) to charge (q)
- **"1/r² for points, 1/r for lines, constant for sheets"** — Field variation
- **"Dipole field is half at equator, axis is double"** — Relative magnitudes
- **"Zero inside hollow sphere, increases linearly in solid sphere"** — Inside sphere

### Key Differences to Remember
- Point charge: E ∝ 1/r²
- Dipole: E ∝ 1/r³
- Line charge: E ∝ 1/r
- Plane sheet: E = constant

### Critical Warnings
⚠️ Don't put signs in Coulomb's formula — use magnitude, then determine direction separately!
⚠️ Gauss's Law only works for symmetric situations!
⚠️ Field inside conductor = 0 in electrostatic equilibrium!
⚠️ Flux is scalar but calculated via vector dot product!
