## Mnemonic: "P.E.N. - Positive means Electron deficiency, Negative means Excess"

### Full Form:
**P**ositive → **E**lectron **N**egative (deficiency!)
**N**egative → **E**lectron **P**ositive (excess!)

### When to Use:
- Remembering what positive/negative charge means in terms of electrons
- Understanding why positive charge attracts negative and vice versa

## Association Techniques

### Linking Electric Field to Gravity
- Just as mass creates gravitational field, charge creates electric field
- Both follow inverse square law
- But gravity only attracts, electric can attract/repel

### Linking Dipole to Small Bar Magnet
- Similar field shape
- Like poles repel, unlike attract
- North pole ≈ positive, South pole ≈ negative (convention)

### Linking Capacitor to Water Tank
- Water level = potential
- Water volume = charge
- Tank area = capacitance
- More area (A) → more capacity

---

## Visual Anchors

### Field Lines as Spider Web
- Imagine positive charge as center of spider web
- Lines radiating outward like web strands
- Closer at center = stronger field

### Dipole as See-Saw
- Pivot at center
- Equal and opposite forces create torque
- Wants to rotate to balanced position

### Gauss's Law as Water Flow
- Imagine charge as water source
- Flow through any closed surface = same
- Size of surface doesn't change total flow
