## Mistake Category 1: Unit Conversion Errors

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Using μC directly in formulas without converting to coulombs
> **Why it Happens:** Students forget that SI units require base units
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Convert μC to C: multiply by 10⁻⁶
> 2. Convert cm to m: divide by 100
> 3. Make conversion the FIRST step
> **Correct Approach:** Always convert to base units (C, m) before calculation

### Examples:
- 5 μC = 5 × 10⁻⁶ C
- 20 cm = 0.2 m
- 5 mm = 0.005 m

## Mistake Category 2: Sign Handling in Coulomb's Law

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Putting negative signs directly into magnitude formula
> **Why it Happens:** Confusion between magnitude and direction
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Always take absolute values in formula
> 2. Determine direction separately using sign inspection
> 3. Remember: Like repel, unlike attract
> **Correct Approach:** F = k|q₁q₂|/r², then decide direction

### Examples:
- For q₁ = +2 μC, q₂ = +3 μC, r = 0.3m:
  - Magnitude: F = 9×10⁹ × 2×10⁻⁶ × 3×10⁻⁶ / 0.3² = 0.6 N
  - Direction: Repulsive (both positive) — away from each other

## Mistake Category 3: Confusing Electric Field with Force

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Writing E = F or E = qF instead of E = F/q
> **Why it Happens:** Not understanding what electric field represents
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Field = Force per unit charge
> 2. Remember: E = F/q, so F = qE
> 3. Units: N/C = Force/Charge
> **Correct Approach:** E is property of source, not dependent on test charge

### Examples:
- Correct: E = 10⁵ N/C, then F on 2 μC = 2×10⁻⁶ × 10⁵ = 0.2 N
- Wrong: E = F means 10⁵ N = F

## Mistake Category 4: Wrong Gaussian Surface Selection

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Using Gauss's Law for asymmetric charge distributions
> **Why it Happens:** Gauss's Law only simplifies when symmetry allows constant E on surface
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Only use when high symmetry: spherical, cylindrical, planar
> 2. Choose surface where E is constant or perpendicular
> 3. If can't simplify integral, don't force Gauss's Law
> **Correct Approach:** Use superposition for non-symmetric cases

---

## Mistake Category 5: Direction Errors in Field

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Thinking field points toward positive charge
> **Why it Happens:** Misunderstanding definition of field direction
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Field direction = direction of force on POSITIVE test charge
> 2. Positive source → field points AWAY
> 3. Negative source → field points TOWARD
> **Correct Approach:** Draw field lines to visualize

## Mistake Category 6: Forgetting Inverse Square vs Inverse

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Using E ∝ 1/r for point charges (should be 1/r²)
> **Why it Happens:** Confusion between different charge distributions
> **How to Avoid:**
> - Point charge: E ∝ 1/r²
> - Line charge: E ∝ 1/r
> - Plane sheet: E = constant
> **Correct Approach:** Match the correct dependence to the charge type

---

## Mistake Category 7: Vector Addition Errors

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Adding field magnitudes as scalars
> **Why it Happens:** Forgetting field is a vector quantity
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Always add fields as vectors
> 2. Use components or geometry
> 3. Check symmetry — often simplifies to zero or simple result
> **Correct Approach:** Break into x, y components, add, then find resultant

---

## Mistake Category 8: Dipole Field Confusion

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Using point charge formula for dipole problems
> **Why it Happens:** Not recognizing dipole case
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Identify if system is dipole (equal opposite charges, small separation)
> 2. For dipole far field: E ∝ 1/r³ (not 1/r²)
> 3. Axis and equatorial field are different magnitudes
> **Correct Approach:** Use p = q×2a, then E = 2p/r³ (axis) or p/r³ (equator)

---

## Mistake Category 9: Inside/Outside Sphere Confusion

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Using E = kQ/r² inside charged sphere
> **Why it Happens:** Not distinguishing hollow vs solid sphere
> **How to Avoid:**
> - Hollow thin shell: E = 0 inside (for r < R)
> - Solid sphere (uniform): E increases linearly: E = kQr/R³ inside
> - Solid sphere outside: E = kQ/r² (like point charge)
> **Correct Approach:** Identify charge distribution first

---

## Mistake Category 10: Flux Sign Errors

> [!COMMON-MISTAKE]
> **Error:** Taking flux as always positive
> **Why it Happens:** Not considering direction of field relative to surface normal
> **How to Avoid:**
> 1. Use formula Φ = E·S = ES cosθ
> 2. For closed surface, outward normal is positive
> 3. Field outward = positive, field inward = negative
> **Correct Approach:** For closed surface, flux through one face may be negative
