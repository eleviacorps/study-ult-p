## FC1. Electric Charge Definition
**Topic:** Electric Charge
**Subtopic:** Definition
**Type:** Conceptual

### Question:
> What is electric charge?

### Answer:
> Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter that causes it to experience electromagnetic forces. It comes in two types: positive and negative. Like charges repel, unlike charges attract.

### Formula:
$$q = ne$$

### Variable Meanings:
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| q | Total charge |
| n | Integer |
| e | 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C |

### When to Use:
- ✅ Any charged object
- ✅ Quantization problems

### When NOT to Use:
- ❌ At quark level (fractional charges)

### Memory Trick:
> [!TIP]
> "P.E.N." — Positive means Electron deficiency, Negative means Electron excess!

## FC2. Coulomb's Law
**Topic:** Coulomb's Law
**Subtopic:** Force formula
**Type:** Formula

### Question:
> State Coulomb's law

### Answer:
> The force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

### Formula:
$$F = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$$

### Variable Meanings:
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| k | 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C² |
| q₁, q₂ | Charges |
| r | Distance |

### When to Use:
- ✅ Point charges
- ✅ Simple force calculations

### When NOT to Use:
- ❌ Extended bodies (need integration)

## FC3. Electric Field
**Topic:** Electric Field
**Subtopic:** Definition
**Type:** Conceptual

### Question:
> Define electric field

### Answer:
> Electric field at a point is the force experienced by a unit positive test charge placed at that point, assuming the test charge doesn't disturb the source charge distribution.

### Formula:
$$\mathbf{E} = \frac{\mathbf{F}}{q}$$

### When to Use:
- ✅ Finding force on charges
- ✅ Understanding field patterns

### Common Confusion:
⚠️ Don't confuse field with force — field is force per unit charge!

## FC5. Superposition Principle
**Topic:** Electric Field
**Subtopic:** Multiple charges
**Type:** Formula

### Question:
> How do you find total electric field from multiple charges?

### Answer:
> Electric field is a vector quantity. Total field is the vector sum of individual fields from all charges.

### Formula:
$$\mathbf{E}_{total} = \sum \mathbf{E}_i$$

### Memory Trick:
> Add as VECTORS, not scalars!

## FC6. Gauss's Law
**Topic:** Gauss's Law
**Subtopic:** Flux
**Type:** Conceptual

### Question:
> State Gauss's Law

### Answer:
> The total electric flux through a closed surface is equal to 1/ε₀ times the total charge enclosed by the surface.

### Formula:
$$\Phi = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\varepsilon_0}$$

### When to Use:
- ✅ Symmetric charge distributions

### Common Mistake:
⚠️ Only enclosed charge matters, not outside charges!

## FC7. Dipole Moment
**Topic:** Electric Dipole
**Subtopic:** Definition
**Type:** Formula

### Question:
> What is dipole moment of an electric dipole?

### Answer:
> Dipole moment is the product of charge and separation, directed from negative to positive charge.

### Formula:
$$\mathbf{p} = q \times 2a$$

### Variable Meanings:
| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| p | Dipole moment (C·m) |
| q | Charge |
| 2a | Separation |

### Memory Trick:
> p = q × d, direction from -q to +q!

**Type:** Value

## FC33. Zero Torque
**Topic:** Dipole in Uniform Field
**Subtopic:** Torque
**Type:** Value

### Question:
> When is torque zero?

### Answer:

> sin 0° = 0, sin 180° = 0

### Stable vs Unstable:
- 0° = stable equilibrium
- 180° = unstable equilibrium

## FC35. Maximum Potential Energy

## FC41. Potential Inside Sphere
**Topic:** Electric Potential
**Subtopic:** Sphere
**Type:** Formula

### Question:
> Electric potential inside uniformly charged sphere?

### Formula:
$$V = \frac{kQ}{2R^3}(3R^2 - r^2)$$

$$V_{center} = \frac{3kQ}{2R}$$

### At Surface (r = R):
$$V_{surface} = \frac{kQ}{R}$$

## FC42. Continuous Charge: Ring
**Topic:** Continuous Charge Distribution
**Subtopic:** Ring
**Type:** Formula

### Question:

### Formula:

### At Far Distance:

## FC43. Continuous Charge: Disc

**Subtopic:** Disc
**Type:** Formula

## FC45. Two Plate Formula vs Sheet Formula
**Topic:** Electric Field
**Subtopic:** Comparison
**Type:** Comparison

### Question:
> Why is field between plates (σ/ε₀) double the field from single sheet (σ/2ε₀)?

### Answer:
> Field from each sheet adds up (same direction)
> Two sheets: E = σ/2ε₀ + σ/2ε₀ = σ/ε₀
> Single sheet: E = σ/2ε₀ (points both ways!)

## FC73. Gauss's Law in Dielectrics
**Topic:** Dielectrics
**Subtopic:** Gauss's Law
**Type:** Formula

## FC89. Electric Pressure Formula
**Topic:** Charged Conductor
**Subtopic:** Pressure
**Type:** Derivation

### Question:
> Derive electric pressure formula

### Answer:
$$P = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{qE}{A} = \frac{\sigma A \cdot \sigma/\varepsilon_0}{A} = \frac{\sigma^2}{2\varepsilon_0}$$

### Alternative:
$$P = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$

### Where:
$$E = \sigma/\varepsilon_0$$

## FC90. Energy Density Derivation
**Topic:** Electric Field
**Subtopic:** Energy
**Type:** Derivation

### Question:
> Derive energy density formula

### Answer:
> Start with U = QV/2 for capacitor
> V = Ed, Q = σA, C = ε₀A/d

$$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 A \frac{E^2 d^2}{d^2} = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2(Ad)$$

### Energy Density:
$$u = \frac{U}{Ad} = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$
