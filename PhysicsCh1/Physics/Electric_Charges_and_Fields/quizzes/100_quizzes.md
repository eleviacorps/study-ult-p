## Quiz Section 1: Electric Charge Basics

### Q1. Multiple Choice
> How many types of electric charges exist?

- A) 1
- B) 2 ✅
- C) 3
- D) 4

**Answer:** B — There are exactly two types: positive and negative

### Q2. True/False
> Like charges attract each other.

**Answer:** False — Like charges repel, unlike attract

### Q3. Fill in Blank
> The SI unit of electric charge is _____.

**Answer:** Coulomb (C)

### Q4. Multiple Choice
> Which of these is a property of electric charge?

- A) Quantization
- B) Conservation
- C) Both A and B ✅
- D) None

**Answer:** C — Charge is both quantized and conserved

### Q5. True/False
> Charge can be created in pairs only.

**Answer:** True — Charge is always created in equal positive and negative pairs

### Q6. Fill in Blank
> Elementary charge e = _____

**Answer:** 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C

## Quiz Section 2: Coulomb's Law

### Q7. Multiple Choice
> Coulomb's law states force is proportional to:

- A) r
- B) r²
- C) 1/r
- D) 1/r² ✅

**Answer:** D — F ∝ 1/r²

### Q8. True/False
> Coulomb's constant k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²

**Answer:** True

### Q9. Fill in Blank
> Force is attractive when charges have _____ signs.

**Answer:** Opposite (different)

### Q10. Multiple Choice
> Force between two charges depends on:

- A) Distance only
- B) Product of charges only
- C) Both distance and product of charges ✅
- D) Neither

**Answer:** C — F ∝ q₁q₂/r²

### Q11. True/False
> Coulomb's force is central.

**Answer:** True — Force acts along the line joining the charges

### Q12. Fill in Blank
> Coulomb's constant k = 1/(4πε₀) has value _____

**Answer:** 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²

### Q13. Multiple Choice
> If distance between charges is doubled, force becomes:

- A) 2 times
- B) 4 times
- C) 1/4 ✅
- D) 1/2

**Answer:** C — F ∝ 1/r², so 1/(2)² = 1/4

### Q14. True/False
> Coulomb's law is valid for all distances.

**Answer:** False — Fails at very small (nuclear) and very large distances

### Q15. Fill in Blank
> For like charges, force is _____

**Answer:** Repulsive

## Quiz Section 3: Electric Field

### Q16. Multiple Choice
> Electric field is defined as:

- A) Force/charge ✅
- B) Charge/force
- C) Force × charge
- D) Force + charge

**Answer:** A — E = F/q

### Q17. True/False
> Electric field points away from positive charge.

**Answer:** True

### Q18. Fill in Blank
> Units of electric field are _____ or _____.

**Answer:** N/C or V/m

### Q19. Multiple Choice
> Electric field due to point charge Q at distance r is:

- A) kQ/r
- B) kQ/r² ✅
- C) kQ/r³
- D) kQr²

**Answer:** B — E = kQ/r²

### Q20. True/False
> Electric field is a vector quantity.

**Answer:** True

### Q21. Fill in Blank
> Direction of electric field is direction of force on _____ test charge.

**Answer:** Positive

### Q22. Multiple Choice
> At r → ∞, electric field from point charge becomes:

- A) Infinity
- B) Zero ✅
- C) Maximum
- D) Undefined

**Answer:** B — E → 0 as r → ∞

### Q23. True/False
> Superposition principle applies to electric field.

**Answer:** True — Total field is vector sum

### Q24. Fill in Blank
> Electric field of dipole falls as _____

**Answer:** 1/r³

## Quiz Section 4: Gauss's Law

### Q25. Multiple Choice
> Gauss's law relates electric flux to:

- A) Distance
- B) Area
- C) Enclosed charge ✅
- D) External charge

**Answer:** C — Φ = q/ε₀

### Q26. True/False
> Electric field inside a charged spherical shell is always zero.

**Answer:** True — For thin shell, E = 0 inside

### Q27. Fill in Blank
> Field due to infinite plane sheet is _____ of distance.

**Answer:** Independent

### Q28. Multiple Choice
> Gaussian surface for line charge should be:

- A) Sphere
- B) Cylinder ✅
- C) Cube
- D) Plane

**Answer:** B — Cylinder gives cylindrical symmetry

### Q29. True/False
> Only enclosed charge contributes to flux in Gauss's law.

**Answer:** True

### Q30. Fill in Blank
> Electric flux is maximum when field is _____ to surface.

**Answer:** Perpendicular

### Q31. Multiple Choice
> Field at distance r from infinite line charge λ:

- A) λ/(2πε₀r) ✅
- B) λ/(ε₀r)
- C) λr/(2πε₀)
- D) λ/(4πε₀r²)

**Answer:** A — E = λ/(2πε₀r)

### Q32. True/False
> Gauss's law can be derived from Coulomb's law.

**Answer:** True — Inverse square law leads to Gauss's law

### Q33. Fill in Blank
> For sphere, Gaussian surface is _____

**Answer:** Sphere (concentric)

## Quiz Section 5: Electric Dipole

### Q34. Multiple Choice
> Dipole moment is:

- A) q/2a
- B) 2aq ✅
- C) q + 2a
- D) q - 2a

**Answer:** B — p = 2aq = q × separation

### Q35. True/False
> Dipole field falls as 1/r³ at large distances.

**Answer:** True — Slower than point charge (1/r²)

### Q36. Fill in Blank
> Torque on dipole in uniform field is maximum at _____ degrees.

**Answer:** 90° (τ = pE sin90° = pE)

### Q37. Multiple Choice
> Direction of dipole moment is from:

- A) Positive to negative
- B) Negative to positive ✅
- C) Perpendicular to line joining charges
- D) None

**Answer:** B — From -q to +q

### Q38. True/False
> On equatorial plane of dipole, potential is zero.

**Answer:** True — V = p cosθ/(4πε₀r²) = 0 when θ = 90°

### Q39. Fill in Blank
> Dipole in non-uniform field experiences _____

**Answer:** Both torque and force

### Q40. Multiple Choice
> Field on axis of dipole (r >> a) is:

- A) p/(4πε₀r³)
- B) 2p/(4πε₀r³) ✅
- C) p/(2πε₀r³)
- D) p/(8πε₀r³)

**Answer:** B — Axial field is twice equatorial field

### Q41. Multiple Choice

- A) Maximum
- B) Zero ✅

- D) 2EA

### Q42. True/False
> Flux is a scalar quantity.

**Answer:** True — Though calculated via surface integral

### Q43. Fill in Blank

**Answer:** q/ε₀

### Q44. Multiple Choice
> Electric flux depends on:

- A) Field strength
- B) Area
- C) Orientation
- D) All of these ✅

**Answer:** D — Φ = EA cosθ

### Q45. True/False
> Gauss's law is valid for any closed surface.

**Answer:** True

### Q46. Fill in Blank
> Flux entering a closed surface is taken as _____

**Answer:** Negative
