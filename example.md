## Q1. Testis Location — Temperature Regulation
**Topic:** [[Male Reproductive System]]
**Subtopic:** Scrotum
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
The testes in humans are situated outside the abdominal cavity inside a pouch called scrotum. The primary purpose served is:

| A | Protect testes from injury | Wrong — protection is secondary, not primary |
| B | Maintain scrotal temperature lower than internal body temp | CORRECT — 2-2.5°C lower for spermatogenesis |
| C | Provide space for epididymis growth | Wrong — epididymis is along testis posterior surface |
| D | Exhibit male secondary sexual characteristic | Wrong — that's a secondary feature |

### Answer: B - Scrotum maintains 2-2.5°C lower temperature for spermatogenesis

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> The scrotum's primary function is thermoregulation. Spermatogenesis requires a temperature 2-2.5°C below normal body temperature (37°C). The dartos muscle (wrinkles skin) and cremaster muscle (raises/lowers testes) along with the pampiniform plexus (countercurrent heat exchange) maintain this optimal temperature of ~33-34°C.

### Why Other Options Are Wrong:
- **A:** While protection is a secondary benefit, NEET specifically asks about the PRIMARY purpose
- **C:** The epididymis fits along the posterior surface regardless of scrotal placement
- **D:** Secondary sexual characteristics are mediated by testosterone, not by scrotum presence

### NEET Insight:
> [!SHORTCUT]
> Any question about scrotum function → answer is temperature regulation

### Common Trap:
⚠️ Students choose "protection" as the answer because it seems intuitive. NEET specifically tests the temperature regulation aspect.

## Q2. Sperm Transport Sequence
**Topic:** [[Male Reproductive System]]
**Subtopic:** Accessory Ducts
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
Which of the following is the correct sequence of sperm transport in the male reproductive system?

| A | Seminiferous tubules → Vasa efferentia → Rete testis → Epididymis → Vas deferens → Ejaculatory duct → Urethra | Wrong — rete testis comes before vasa efferentia |
| B | Testis → Epididymis → Vasa efferentia → Vas deferens → Ejaculatory duct → Inguinal canal → Urethra | Wrong — order of epididymis and vasa efferentia is reversed |
| C | Seminiferous tubules → Rete testis → Vasa efferentia → Epididymis → Vas deferens → Ejaculatory duct → Urethra → Urethral meatus | CORRECT |
| D | Seminiferous tubules → Rete testis → Epididymis → Vasa efferentia → Vas deferens → Ejaculatory duct → Urethra | Wrong — vasa efferentia comes before epididymis |

### Answer: C - Correct sequence

### Detailed Explanation:
**Step-by-step:**
1. Sperms produced in **seminiferous tubules**
2. Collected by **rete testis** (network at hilum)
3. Exit testis via **vasa efferentia** (15-20 ductules)
4. Enter **epididymis** for maturation and storage
5. Transported by **vas deferens**
6. Vas deferens + seminal vesicle duct → **ejaculatory duct**
7. Passes through **urethra**
8. Exits through **urethral meatus**

### Why Other Options Are Wrong:
- **A:** Rete testis comes BEFORE vasa efferentia
- **B:** The sequence skips rete testis and has wrong order
- **D:** Vasa efferentia come BEFORE epididymis

### NEET Insight:
> [!SHORTCUT]
> Mnemonic: "Seminiferous tubes Really Very Efficiently Store Viable Ejaculate" = Seminiferous → Rete → Vasa efferentia → Epididymis → Vas deferens → Ejaculatory duct

**Topic:** [[Male Reproductive System]]
**Subtopic:** Testes — Interstitial Cells

### Problem:
Leydig cells are found in the testes and secrete:

- **D:** GnRH is produced by the hypothalamus

## Q4. Sertoli Cells Function
**Topic:** [[Male Reproductive System]]
**Subtopic:** Seminiferous Tubules
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
Which of the following is NOT a function of Sertoli cells?

| A | Provide nutrition to developing germ cells | Wrong — this IS a function of Sertoli cells |
| B | Secrete testosterone | CORRECT — testosterone is secreted by Leydig cells, NOT Sertoli cells |
| C | Form blood-testis barrier | Wrong — this IS a function of Sertoli cells |
| D | Secrete inhibin | Wrong — this IS a function of Sertoli cells |

### Answer: B - Secrete testosterone

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> Sertoli cells (also called sustentacular cells) are found inside the seminiferous tubules. Their functions include:
> - Providing nutrition to germ cells
> - Forming the blood-testis barrier via tight junctions
> - Secreting inhibin (negative feedback on FSH)
> - Secreting androgen-binding protein (ABP) to concentrate testosterone
> - Phagocytosing residual bodies during spermiogenesis
> - **They do NOT produce testosterone** — that's the function of Leydig cells

### Why Other Options Are Wrong:
- **A, C, D:** These are all genuine functions of Sertoli cells

### NEET Insight:
> [!SHORTCUT]
> Sertoli cells = Support cells. They SUPPORT spermatogenesis but don't produce hormones.

## Q7. Primary Spermatocyte Chromosome Number
**Topic:** [[Gametogenesis]]
**Subtopic:** Spermatogenesis
**Difficulty:** Easy
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
A primary spermatocyte has how many chromosomes?

| A | 23 | Wrong — that's haploid (spermatid/sperm) |
| B | 46 | CORRECT — primary spermatocyte is diploid |
| C | 92 | Wrong — that would be tetraploid |
| D | 69 | Wrong |

### Answer: B - 46 chromosomes (2n)

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> Primary spermatocytes are diploid (2n=46). They are formed from spermatogonia (also 2n=46) after growth phase. They undergo meiosis I (reduction division) to form two haploid secondary spermatocytes (n=23). Note: After S phase, primary spermatocytes have 4C DNA content but still 46 chromosomes (each chromosome has 2 sister chromatids).

## Q9. Meiosis II Completion in Female
**Topic:** [[Gametogenesis]]
**Subtopic:** Oogenesis

### Problem:
In human females, meiosis II is completed:

| A | At birth | Wrong — oocytes are arrested at prophase I at birth |
| B | At puberty | Wrong — first meiotic division completes just before ovulation |
| C | At ovulation | Wrong — secondary oocyte is arrested at metaphase II |
| D | After fertilisation | CORRECT |

### Answer: D - After fertilisation

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> The secondary oocyte released at ovulation is arrested at **metaphase II**. Meiosis II is completed ONLY if fertilisation occurs. The sperm penetration triggers the completion of meiosis II, producing a haploid ovum (ootid) and a second polar body.

### Why Other Options Are Wrong:
- **A:** At birth, primary oocytes are arrested at prophase I
- **B:** The first meiotic division completes just before ovulation at puberty
- **C:** At ovulation, the secondary oocyte is arrested at metaphase II, not completed

## Q13. Fertilisation Block to Polyspermy

| B | Zona pellucida changes (cortical reaction) | CORRECT |
| C | Sertoli cell barrier | Wrong — that's in testes |
| D | LH surge | Wrong — that triggers ovulation |

### Answer: B - Changes in zona pellucida after the first sperm enters

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]

> **Spermatogenesis:** Complete process from spermatogonium to sperm

## Q98. Identical vs Fraternal Twins
**Topic:** [[Multiple Topics]]
**Subtopic:** Fertilisation
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
A boy and a girl twin are born. Which of the following must be true?

| A | They are monozygotic twins | Wrong — monozygotic twins are same sex |
| B | They are dizygotic (fraternal) twins | CORRECT |
| C | They were conceived through IVF | Wrong |
| D | They have 75% identical genetic content | Wrong — fraternal twins share ~50% like siblings |

### Answer: B - They are fraternal (dizygotic) twins

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> **Monozygotic (identical) twins:** One zygote splits → same genetic material → always same sex.
> **Dizygotic (fraternal) twins:** Two eggs + two sperms → different genetic material → can be same or different sexes.
> Since a boy and a girl have different sex chromosomes (XY vs XX), they must be fraternal twins.

## Q99. Hormonal Feedback During Menstrual Cycle
**Topic:** [[Menstrual Cycle]]
**Subtopic:** Feedback Mechanisms
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Exam:** NEET UG

### Problem:
Which of the following correctly describes the feedback effect of estrogen on LH and FSH secretion?

| A | Estrogen always inhibits LH and FSH | Wrong |
| B | Low levels of estrogen inhibit while high levels stimulate LH/FSH | CORRECT |
| C | Estrogen has no effect on LH and FSH | Wrong |
| D | Estrogen only stimulates FSH | Wrong |

### Answer: B - Low levels of estrogen inhibit (negative feedback), while high levels stimulate (positive feedback) LH and FSH

### Detailed Explanation:
> [!KEY-CONCEPT]
> **Estrogen's dual feedback:**
> - During early follicular phase: Low/moderate estrogen → **negative feedback** (suppresses FSH, LH)
> - Just before ovulation: High estrogen (~day 12-13) → **positive feedback** (stimulates LH surge and FSH surge)
> - This LH surge triggers ovulation
